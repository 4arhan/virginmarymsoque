Why “Virgin Mary Mosque”?

Maryam is the only woman named in the Qur’an. She is spoken of in the highest terms — and the 19th chapter carries her name. Our mosque exists in that light.

Her name in the Qur’an

Maryam (Mary, mother of ‘Isa / Jesus — peace be upon them) is named thirty-four times in the Qur’an. Surah 19 (Maryam) narrates her birth, her seclusion, the miraculous conception, and the defence her infant son offered of her chastity.

“O Maryam, indeed Allah has chosen you and purified you and chosen you above the women of the worlds.”
— Qur’an 3:42

A bridge, not a boundary

For Christians, Mary is the mother of the Messiah. For Muslims, Maryam is the virtuous servant of Allah whose son ‘Isa is the Messiah, a prophet and a word from God.

Naming our mosque for her is not a conflation of traditions; it is an affirmation of what is shared. Both communities hold her in reverence. A building bearing her name should, by that fact alone, be a place of dialogue.

What the name asks of us

  • Worship: we are, first, a mosque — five daily prayers, Jumu’ah, Eid, and Qur’anic learning.
  • Hospitality: visitors are welcomed, not tolerated. Our doors are open for tours, questions, and interfaith visits.
  • Integrity: we teach Maryam’s story as the Qur’an teaches it, without softening the points of difference between traditions.
  • Dignity: women’s spaces in our mosque are generous, safe, and visible — because a mosque named for a woman can settle for nothing less.

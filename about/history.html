Our history

From a rented community hall to a purpose-built masjid — and the story of the name we carry.

2008

A rented hall

A small group of families begins holding Friday prayers in a rented community hall in inner-north Melbourne.

2012

First musallah

A dedicated prayer space is leased. Weekly youth halaqas and women’s classes begin.

2016

Incorporation

Incorporated as a not-for-profit. The board is established. First interfaith dialogue event hosted.

2019

Named for Maryam

After community consultation, the congregation adopts the name “Virgin Mary Mosque / Masjid Maryam” to honour Maryam (peace be upon her) and to signal an ongoing commitment to interfaith engagement.

2022

Land acquired

Through community fundraising, land is purchased for the purpose-built mosque.

Today

A growing masjid

A congregation of several hundred families, a full weekly programme, and regular interfaith events with Christian and Jewish neighbours across Melbourne.

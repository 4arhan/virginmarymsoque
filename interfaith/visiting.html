Visiting the mosque

Thinking of visiting for the first time? Here is what to expect, in plain language.

You are welcome

You do not need to be Muslim to step inside. School groups, parishioners, journalists, students and curious individuals come every week. Our ushers will greet you at the door and answer practical questions. If you tell them it is your first visit, they will give you a short tour.

What to wear

Dress modestly — long trousers or a long skirt, and a shirt with sleeves that covers the shoulders. Women are asked to cover their hair with a scarf inside the prayer hall. If you don’t have one, we have clean scarves available to borrow at the entrance.

Everyone removes their shoes before entering the carpeted prayer areas. Shoe racks are provided.

Where to enter

The main entrance welcomes everyone. Women may continue to the women’s entrance for direct access to the women’s prayer hall; men continue to the main prayer hall.

What happens in a prayer

Islamic prayer involves standing, bowing and prostrating. If you are visiting, you are welcome to sit quietly at the back of the hall and watch respectfully. There is no pressure to participate. The prayer is short — usually 5–10 minutes — and is led by the Imam.

On Fridays, the Imam gives a sermon (khutbah) in English (or Arabic with English summary) before the congregational prayer.

Taking photographs

Photographs of the architecture are welcome. Please do not photograph people during prayer. If you are on assignment, please speak to the office first.

Food and drink

No food or drink inside the prayer hall (except water). Tea and biscuits are usually available in the foyer after Friday prayer — you are welcome to stay.

Booking a group visit

For school classes, parishes, university chaplaincies, or any group of more than six, please book in advance so we can have a guide ready for you. Arrange a visit

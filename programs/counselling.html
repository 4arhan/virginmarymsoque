Counselling & chaplaincy

Confidential pastoral conversations with the Imam or a trained chaplain — in person, by phone or online.

When to reach out

  • Struggles with faith, prayer or doubt
  • Family or marital tension
  • Grief and bereavement
  • Anxiety, depression, or low mood
  • Substance use or gambling
  • Family violence (see below)
  • Identity, belonging, and life transitions

Conversations are confidential. We will refer to a clinical psychologist or GP where specialist care is the right step.

How to book

Appointments are usually available within a week. For urgent situations we will always find time the same day.

Request an appointment

Chaplaincy in hospitals & prisons

Our Imam and trained volunteers visit Muslim patients and inmates on request. Families, social workers, or facility staff can make a referral.

Family violence

If you are in danger

If you or someone you know is in immediate danger, call 000. For confidential support:

  • Safe Steps (24/7, women): 1800 015 188
  • 1800 RESPECT (24/7, all genders): 1800 737 732
  • Men’s Referral Service: 1300 766 491

Our mosque takes family violence seriously. Our Imam and board have adopted a zero-tolerance statement and have trained with specialist services. You can come to us and be believed.

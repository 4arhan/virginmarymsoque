Interested in Islam?

A short, honest introduction — and an open invitation to ask the rest of your questions in person.

What Muslims believe

Islam rests on six articles of faith:

  1. Belief in Allah — one God, the creator, uncreated, without partners.
  2. Belief in His angels.
  3. Belief in His books — the Qur’an, and earlier scriptures including the Torah, Psalms and Gospel.
  4. Belief in His messengers — including Nuh, Ibrahim, Musa, Dawud, ‘Isa and Muhammad (peace be upon them all).
  5. Belief in the Day of Judgement.
  6. Belief in divine decree — nothing happens outside God’s knowledge and will.

What Muslims do

Islam’s five pillars are the everyday framework of Muslim practice:

  1. Shahada — bearing witness that there is no god but God, and that Muhammad is His messenger.
  2. Salah — the five daily prayers.
  3. Zakat — an annual charitable contribution, calculated on certain wealth.
  4. Sawm — fasting from dawn to sunset in the month of Ramadan.
  5. Hajj — the pilgrimage to Makkah, once in a lifetime for those who are able.

Common questions

Do I have to convert to come to the mosque?

No. You are welcome to visit, observe, and ask questions with no expectation of anything more. See our visiting guide.

What about Jesus (‘Isa)?

Muslims love and honour ‘Isa. He is a prophet and the Messiah, born to Maryam miraculously. See Maryam in Islam.

Isn’t Islam only for Arabs?

No. Most Muslims worldwide are not Arab. Our congregation alone brings together families from 30+ countries of origin, as well as Australian-born Muslims of every background.
